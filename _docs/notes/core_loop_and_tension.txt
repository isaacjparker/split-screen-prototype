## Notes
--------

# Design Principle
Timing / Sequencing (Kingdom) and Space / Resources (Dungeon of the Endless) are two expressions
of the same design principle:
	
	"How much can you safely expand before the next threat arrives, and what do you sacrifice to 
	find out?"

The purpose of the prototype is to find out which of these feels like the way to go. Both support
a co-op dynamic but each one will have differencees in feel and how much it allows for player
expression. It may be that we find a combination of the two but in any case, the question is best
answered by feel and experimentation, not by rigid edesign or theory.

# Progression Structure: Soft Meta Progression
- Persistent village: grows across many sessons, carries identity and history
- New procedural map each run: fresh spatial and resource problem every time.
- Village progression is deliberately slow: Compelling reason to return across sessions.
- Players can develop their village between sessions without friends present
- Resources and recruits brought back from runs feed village growth.
This structure provides a persitent space for player expression while allowing for a reset of
tension each run. This only requires player to hold one mental model at a time - the problem space
during a run, and the creative and expressive space between runs.
