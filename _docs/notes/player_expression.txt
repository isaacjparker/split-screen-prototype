# player_expression
Project : Split Screen Prototype
Created : 2026-03-23
====================

## Notes
--------

1. The Village
- Sandbox layout - player defines position and shape of safe zones
	Alternatively: space as a resource, 'plots' that can be built on and add to the
	space / resource tension. Plots and buildings become a resource and choosing one will
	potentially prevent another. May create more deliberate and unique builds.
- Buildings provide mechanical benefits and NPC housing - NPCs then compound benefits / effects
and create the social juice feedback.
- Building type examples (not set): blacksmith, wizard's tower, shops, houseing, etc.
- The village is a self protrait - reflects personality and play style.
- Village scenes can afford more colour, warmth, and detail than combat zones.
- The village is where character customisation can be appreciated a tclose range. Eg village
buildings will have player's primary and secondary colours.

2. NPC Recruitment
- Recruitable NPCs encourntered on runs, brought back to inhabit the village.
- NPCs are characterful archetypes with distinct personalities.
- They react to player choices, build decisions, and reputation.
- They compound village benefits and effects. Players give resources and jobs to their balcksmith
who levels up, increasing the effectiveness or speciality of their smithing building.
- A lightweight relationship/reputation system tracks key variables.
- Contextual dialogue referencing those variables creates the Fable-like juice.

3. Character Customisation
- Primary expression tool as combat scale: colour scheme (2-3 tones per charcater)
- Silhouette variation through equipment and skill choice.
- Combat build expressed through village progression and equipment choices. More powerful village -
more powerful character.
- Detailed customisation is best appreciated in village scenes, not combat.
- None visual cusstomisation may add social juice. Player titles and wealth (for example) change
social NPC feedback.
