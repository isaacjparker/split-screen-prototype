# core_tensions
Project : Split Screen Prototype
Created : 2026-03-25
====================

## Overview
-----------

Describing the core tensions in different games which inspire this prototype or are related in some way.
These tensions may not be objectively 'correct' but are my thoughts and reflections on the nature of these games.
These thoughts are iterative and may very well change in the future.


## Notes
--------

I feel many strategy and strategy-like games have a core tension involving:
	Time vs Space vs Resources.
Which of these three the game limits or provides in abundance, changes the core tension of that game.

For example, a game can have abundant resources to solve a spatial problem under time pressure.

Examples:

1. Kingdom (Noio)
Abundance: Resources
Limits: Time
To Solve: A spatial problem

Kingdom technically has abundant resources as by the end game, farms will provide infinite coins and
villagers will spawn for ever. With this resource, players manage their play space under time pressure. 
Players cannot sit back and chill in their village, they must be acting at any available moment to use 
their resources to manage their space.


2. Dungeon of the Endless (Amplitude)
Abundance: Time
Limits: Resources
To solve: A spatial problem

DotE feels like a similar game to Kingdom at ffirst glance and in many ways it is; it's a spatial
problem solved through the balance of resources and time.
However, with DotE, resources are limites. In any given level or run, there is no way to sit tight
and earn infinite resources. Meanwhile, the timing and pacing of th egame is entirely up to the player.
The player can take a whole hour to plan their next room-opening if they wish.


3. Thronefall (Jonas Tyroller)
Abundance: Time
Limits: Resources
To solve: A spatial problem

Despite being inspired by Kingdom, Thronefall is much more like DotE. In Thronefall, players do not have
an infinite faucet of money, but as in DotE, they can progress and manage timing at their own pace.
